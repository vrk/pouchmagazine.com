<h2 id="how-we-select">How we select what goes in Pouch magazine</h2>

When we select which submissions will be published in an issue of Pouch, it‚Äôs like we‚Äôre arranging a lovely bouquet of flowers. And to take the metaphor a step further: it‚Äôs a flower *bouquet*, not a flower *competition.*

In other words, when reviewing submissions, Pouch is **not** trying to assess what are ‚Äúthe best‚Äù layouts we‚Äôve received; we don‚Äôt believe in that premise. A glimpse into a person‚Äôs journal is a glimpse into their inner world ‚Äî how could such a thing be ranked? Ah, but we have a magazine to publish! And a magazine requires curation in order to be coherent for its readers. For Pouch Issue 3, we *probably* can‚Äôt publish all submissions, so how will we select what gets published? 

Of course, we will be following the submission criteria outlined above, but beyond that: It is a vague and imprecise science to find a set of layouts that we think work well together within the issue. We‚Äôll put submissions next to each other, move them around, and see what emerges. The final selection will be just *one* arrangement of beautiful flowers, out of all the other equally beautiful permutations possible.

<h2 id="planner-or-journal">‚ÄúIs my submission a planner or a journal layout?‚Äù</h2>

In our submission form, we ask you to categorize your submission as either a ‚Äúplanner‚Äù or a ‚Äújournal‚Äù layout ‚Äî but we know there‚Äôs a lot of ambiguity around the two terms!

For example, are the Hobonichi notebooks ‚Äúplanners‚Äù or ‚Äújournals‚Äù? Many people use their Hobonichi as either a planner, or a journal, or both, or neither. Hobonichi themselves avoid either classification and call their notebooks ‚ÄúLife Books‚Äù for this reason!

That said, even though the *notebooks* might be used for any purpose, we think most *layouts* are created with a specific purpose in mind.

Here is how we‚Äôre defining ‚Äúplanner‚Äù vs ‚Äújournal‚Äù for submissions this time around:

- **Planner layout** = This layout is *primarily* for managing your schedule and tasks, i.e. planning for the future.
    - Examples: Managing your to-do list; keeping track of appointments; project planning; habit tracking; etc.
- **Journal layout** = This layout is *primarily* for memory-keeping, i.e. documenting the past.
    - Examples: Daily journal; logging what books you‚Äôve read; monthly photo dump; collecting ephemera; monthly calendar drawings; gratitude journaling; etc.

For Pouch Issue 3, we are currently accepting submissions for **planners** and **journals** based on these rough definitions above!

### Q&A:
